Machine learning for experimental particle physics

We are the RODEM group at the University of Geneva. We develop deep learning methods for the analysis of data from the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider — building tools that simulate detectors faster, reconstruct events more precisely, and search for new physics without assuming in advance what it looks like.

Research

What we work on

Our work sits between machine learning and collider physics: methods developed here are designed to be deployed on real LHC data.

Anomaly detection

Model-agnostic searches for new physics that do not presuppose a specific signal, using unsupervised and weakly supervised learning to find rare, unexpected structure in collision data.

Generative models

Normalizing flows, diffusion models and autoencoders for fast simulation of particle showers and event generation — replacing expensive Monte Carlo with learned surrogates.

Reconstruction & tagging

Neural approaches to jet physics, heavy-flavour tagging and particle identification, improving how raw detector signals become physics objects.

Physics at the LHC

Measurements and searches with the ATLAS detector — top quark and Higgs boson properties, and direct searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
